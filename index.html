Work that stays

Punkeye Pictures is a disability-led community media social enterprise in Vancouver. We support adults with developmental disabilities to author films and video over time, alongside caregivers and day-program teams, using tools people already know.

2026 programs in progress

Our current 2026 implementation is underway with partner organizations, with staff training, capture setup, archive practice, and participant-led story development happening in real programs.

If your organization wants a future phase, capacity-building support, or scoped implementation help, we welcome the conversation.

We build media practice that survives staff turnover and keeps participants in creative control.

How it works Book an intro call

Art for the timeline, not the landfill

In many programs, daily creative work disappears at week’s end. At Punkeye, every photo, sketch, and clip is treated as a permanent building block. We build accessible production routines so daily effort becomes durable participant-led work, not disposable activity.

A way in

The filmmaking world has long been guarded by gates that are difficult for many to open. Punkeye Pictures is designed to be a way in.

Different paths

Not everyone follows the same road. For some, this becomes a lifelong creative practice. For others, it's the professional foundation they've been denied—a place to hone skills, build a portfolio, grow into themselves as authors and filmmakers. We don't run people through training pipelines. We give them structure and time, and let talent emerge at its own pace.

Everyone belongs

The work is built from small pieces—one frame, one choice, one clip. No one is excluded by the format. You don't have to match anyone else's pace; contributions stack over days and weeks, and each one still counts. One frame at a time.

Why Punkeye Pictures exists

Too many people with disabilities get offered activities that pass time but do not build momentum, authorship, or memory.

Punkeye is built as an alternative: structured creative work that compounds over weeks and months. Creative value is not who holds the camera; it is who makes decisions. Our role is to make authorship possible with accessible tools, clear routines, and respect for each person’s pace and consent.
