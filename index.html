From one-off to ongoing

Daily craft projects aren't disposable here. Each piece feeds into an ongoing work—something that builds over time.

Why Punkeye Pictures exists

Too many people with disabilities end up in programs that fill time instead of building it. Same thing every day. No sense of moving forward.

We're building something different: structured creative work. People contribute to media projects—short films, video, digital art—over weeks and months. The focus is routine, purpose, and the satisfaction of making something that lasts.

Authorship over output

Creative value isn't defined by who holds the camera. It's defined by who makes the decisions and keeps the project going.

Continuity over novelty

Projects unfold over time. Participants experience weeks and months moving forward—not looping back to the same one-off activities.

Every body has a story

We believe everyone has a story worth telling. Our job is to make it possible to tell it—using tools that work for each person.

For families, organizations, and funders

We work with caregivers, day programs, and families. We enhance what's already there—we don't replace it. Phones and simple tools that staff already have. The aim isn't perfection; it's participation, confidence, and something to show for it.
