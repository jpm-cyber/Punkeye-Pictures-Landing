What we do

Phones, simple tools, structure. Projects that build over time.

Program types

We offer structured media projects that fit different settings and goals:

  • Participant-led short-film series — Participants shape the story; caregivers support with phones and simple tools.
  • Caregiver-facilitated media modules — Day programs run the model with training and oversight from us.
  • Phone-based video projects — No complex equipment. Daily creative tasks build into finished films over 4–8 weeks.

Who this is for

Day programs, families, schools, and community organizations serving people with developmental disabilities.

How it works

Intake → skill-mapping → 4–8 week project cycle → screening or share. We adapt the timeline to your program.

High-End Production, Zero-Fuss Tech

Our system takes care of the boring parts of making films—the sorting, filing, and managing of media. This allows caregivers and participants to stay focused on the creative process. We handle the technical side so they can focus on the story.

Low barrier, high quality

Participants use smartphones and simple digital tools. Everyone can contribute from day one.

Daily goals, real projects

We break big projects into small, manageable tasks. One simple creative task per day, done well.

Universal Participation

We don't assign or train people for specific roles. Participants contribute in whatever ways work for them, ensuring no one is left behind.

Paced, not pressured

Projects unfold at the pace that works. No deadlines. Daily structure without external pressure.

For Organizations: Building Assets, Not Waste

We teach caregivers the model, which slots into existing routines without requiring expensive equipment or specialized instructors.

Keeping Art out of the Landfill

Traditional day programs often produce piles of seasonal coloring pages that end up in the bin by Friday afternoon. Punkeye flips this model. Every sheet of paper used becomes a permanent digital frame in a film timeline. We keep art out of the trash and in the project.

Saving on Supplies

Stop spending your budget on "filler" craft kits and disposable coloring books. By using the tools your staff already own and turning daily activities into permanent assets, we lower your supply costs while raising the value of your programming.

Filling the Schedule

We provide a built-in activity that eliminates the constant search for new ways to pass the time. Caregivers won't start each week looking at an empty schedule.
