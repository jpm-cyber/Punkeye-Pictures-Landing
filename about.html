Who we serve

Day-program providers, families, disability-support organizations, and schools. We work with anyone who wants to turn time-filling into something that builds.

The Founder

40+ years in broadcast and film; decades in disability advocacy.

I've spent over 40 years in broadcasting and film—CBC Radio, the National Film Board, independent documentary—and decades in disability advocacy and community support. I also worked as a Community Support Worker and saw what day programs face: staff burnout, schedules that default to time-filling instead of building. I've watched staff clear bins of artwork that took all week to make.

Punkeye Pictures is what happens when those two lives meet. The professional craft of Citizen Sam (NFB, 2006) meets the reality of creating systems that turn passive busywork into work that lasts.

Own Voices

"Own Voices" describes work by people telling their own stories, from their own lived experience, rather than others speaking for them. For disability, it means people with disabilities creating and shaping the narratives about disability. That philosophy matters to me. At Punkeye Pictures, we use technology and structure to create space for people to tell their own stories.

The Punkeye Ethos

The name comes from a childhood acquaintance who stood for independence and humor. It's meant to be raw and a bit defiant—against a system that defaults to filling time.
